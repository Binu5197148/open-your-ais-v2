<h2>AI Takes the Stage at Cannes</h2><p>The Cannes Innovation Award has a new recipient this year: an AI-driven scriptwriting tool. This tool, recognized for its ability to generate narratives and dialogue, promises to reshape the way filmmakers approach storytelling. As someone who has spent 14 years directing and editing commercials for global brands like Disney and Yamaha, I&apos;m both excited and apprehensive about this development.</p><h2>Not the End of Creativity, But a New Chapter</h2><p>Many might fear that AI tools like this one could spell the end of traditional scriptwriting. However, it's not about replacing human creativity. It's about expanding our capabilities. In my experience, AI opens doors that were previously closed due to budget constraints or team limitations. It's not about doing less; it's about doing more with what we have.</p><h2>Democratizing Storytelling</h2><p>This tool can democratize storytelling by giving independent filmmakers the ability to create high-quality scripts. Imagine the range of voices and stories that can now be brought to life. When I founded Pichorra Filmes in São Paulo, I never imagined that such a tool could help level the playing field for small studios like ours.</p><h2>Collaboration Over Replacement</h2><p>AI should be seen as a collaborator, not a competitor. In my career, I&apos;ve worn many hats — composer, actor, editor, writer, director. Each role brings its unique perspective, and AI can be an incredible partner in the creative process. It&apos;s like having a brainstorming partner that never tires, always ready to explore new ideas.</p><h2>The Future of Filmmaking</h2><p>As AI continues to evolve, it will undoubtedly change the landscape of filmmaking. But this change should be embraced, not feared. It amplifies our abilities and offers new opportunities to tell the stories that matter. The Cannes recognition is just the beginning. Let&apos;s embrace this new chapter with open minds.</p><p>AI tools will never replace the human touch in storytelling. But they can help us reach new heights. Let's not resist the change but harness it for a more diverse and inclusive future in filmmaking.</p>
